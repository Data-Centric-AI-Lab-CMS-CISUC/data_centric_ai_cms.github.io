## <img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/lucide-icons/lucide/main/icons/file-text.svg" width="20" height="20" style="vertical-align: middle;"> Abstract
This paper explores the imperative of fairness in federated learning by unveiling group-specific distributed concept drift. We demonstrate how data shifts across different clients can lead to biased model performance and propose strategies to mitigate these effects.

## <img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/lucide-icons/lucide/main/icons/quote.svg" width="20" height="20" style="vertical-align: middle;"> Full Citation
> Teresa Salazar, João Gama, Helder Araújo, Pedro Henriques Abreu, **"Unveiling group-specific distributed concept drift: A fairness imperative in federated learning"**, *IEEE Transactions on Neural Networks and Learning Systems*, 37(1), 161-175, 2025.
